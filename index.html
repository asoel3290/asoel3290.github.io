How safe is southern Florida of sea level rise?

With the inevitable rise in sea levels, many coastal areas are at risk of inundation.

Florida, famously known for its low elevation, is one of the most at risk states in the United States. Being aware of the risks of sea level rise in counties of southern Florida is important as lives of hundreds of thousands of people will be affected.

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) has developed three sea level rise scenarios: low, intermediate, and high.

Tell me more!

Sea Level Rise Scenarios

low: assumes nations successfully limit global warming to less than 2 degrees Celcius and ice loss is limited. Sea level rise is projected to rise about 1.6 feet by 2100

intermediate: assumes global carbon emissions rise and then decline by the middle of the century, and ice sheets melt at rates in line with historical observations. Sea level rise is projected to rise about 4 feet by 2100

high: assumes a continued rise in global carbon emissions and increasing loss of land ice. Sea level rise is projected to rise about 6.5 feet by 2100
